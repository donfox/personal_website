Books Related to Computing

Favorite Books Related to Computing

  • Gödel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid
    Douglas R. Hofstadter

    This book explores the "hard problem" of consciousness—how a self can arise from non-self entities. Hofstadter presents key concepts in meta-mathematics and theoretical computer science from a unique perspective.

    Watch the MIT Lecture on Gödel, Escher, Bach on YouTube.

  • The Pragmatic Programmer: From Journeyman to Master
    Andrew Hunt, David Thomas

    This book offers essential tips and best practices for software development, helping programmers enhance their problem-solving skills and deepen their understanding of the craft.

  • Pragmatic Thinking & Learning: Refactor Your Wetware
    Andy Hunt

    Expanding on The Pragmatic Programmer, this book delves into techniques from cognitive science and learning theory, offering valuable methods for refining programming skills.

  • Land Of Lisp: Learn to Program LISP, One Game at a Time
    Conrad Barski M.D.

    This playful Lisp tutorial makes complex concepts accessible and enjoyable, even for those with prior knowledge of the language. Companion books in the same style cover Haskell and Erlang.

    Video: Land of Lisp Introduction

Mathematics Books I Like

  • Good Math: A Geek's Guide to the Beauty of Numbers, Logic, and Computation
    Mark C. Chu-Carroll

    This book offers a fascinating journey through number theory, logic, set theory, and more. Mark's blog, Good Math/Bad Math, complements the content.

  • Burn Math Class: And Reinvent Mathematics for Yourself
    Jason Wilkes

    This book encourages a fresh perspective on mathematical concepts by reconstructing them.

  • Everything and More: A Compact History of Infinity
    David Foster Wallace

    A unique exploration of metamathematics, centered around the abstraction of infinity.

  • International Journal of Mathematics and Consciousness

    This journal delves into the intersection of mathematics and consciousness, offering hard copies and downloadable articles on their website.

  • Laws of Form
    G. Spencer-Brown

    An intriguing book presenting a 'calculus of indications,' exploring basic forms underlying multiple sciences.
